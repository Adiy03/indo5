Once the cassava slices are fully dried, they become hard and brittle, with a lower moisture content compared to fresh cassava

It can be rehydrated by soaking in water or other liquids, and then cooked or used in various culinary preparations. Dried cassava can be ground into flour to make porridge, pancakes, or other baked goods

Cassava Starch


Cassava starch is a type of starch extracted from the cassava root,
which is also known as yuca, manioc, or tapioca

The process of extracting cassava starch involves washing and peeling the cassava roots, then grating or grinding them into a pulp. The pulp is then mixed with water and sieved to separate the starch from the fibrous residue. The starch is then dried and may be further processed into various forms, such as flour, pearls, or flakes

Cassava starch is widely used in food and industrial applications. In food, it is used as a thickener, binder, and stabilizer in products such as soups, sauces, gravies, and baked goods
