Coconut Sugar


Coconut sugar, also known as coconut palm sugar or coco sugar, is a natural sweetener derived from the sap of coconut palm trees

It is a popular alternative to traditional cane sugar and is commonly used as a healthier and more sustainable sweetener option

It has a caramel-like flavor with a hint of coconut, which can add a unique taste to recipes


Coconut sugar can be used as a substitute for cane sugar in a variety of recipes, including beverages, baked goods, desserts, and savory dishes

It is often used as a one-to-one replacement for cane sugar due to its unique flavor profile

Coconut sugar is often considered more sustainable compared to cane sugar because it is derived from a renewable resourceâ€”the coconut palm tree

Coconut sugar is made by collecting the sap from the flower buds of coconut palm trees

Farmers make incisions in the bud stalks and collect the sap that drips out. The sap is then heated to evaporate the moisture, leaving behind granules or blocks of coconut sugar

Coconut sugar has gained popularity among those seeking low-glycemic sweeteners
