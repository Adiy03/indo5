Fish maw is the dried swim bladder of certain species of fish, mainly from the sea or freshwater sources. It is removed from the fish, thoroughly cleaned, and then dried to produce the final product, which is known as fish maw

Dried fish maw has a distinctive appearance, often resembling a light, porous, and translucent, yet firm, texture. Before use, it needs to be soaked in water to rehydrate and soften, enabling it to absorb flavors during cooking

Fish maw is highly valued for its unique texture and ability to absorb flavors from the ingredients it is cooked with. It is commonly used in various dishes such as soups, stews, and braised dishes, where it adds a gelatinous and rich texture.
