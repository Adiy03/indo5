Dried Fish Maw for Sale


Fish, dried, even salted but not smoked, HS Code: 030559

Dried fish maw is a seafood delicacy commonly used in various culinary traditions, particularly in East and Southeast Asian cuisines

Fish maw is the dried swim bladder of certain species of fish, mainly from the sea or freshwater sources. It is removed from the fish, thoroughly cleaned, and then dried to produce the final product, which is known as fish maw

Dried Fish Maw Uses:


Soups: Dried fish maw is often used to enhance the texture and flavor of soups. It has a gelatinous quality that thickens broths, providing a rich and hearty consistency. Fish maw is a popular addition to traditional Chinese soups, such as shark fin soup replacements or fish maw soup

Stews and Braised Dishes: Fish maw can be added to stews and braised dishes, absorbing the flavors of the surrounding ingredients. It becomes tender when simmered, making it a delightful addition to dishes like braised sea cucumber with fish maw

Dim Sum: Fish maw is sometimes used as a filling in dim sum dishes. It can be combined with other ingredients such as mushrooms, bamboo shoots, and seasonings to create delicious dumplings or spring rolls

Congee: In Chinese cuisine, fish maw is occasionally added to congee (rice porridge) for both its texture and nutritional value. The congee takes on the infused flavors of the fish maw during the cooking process

Stir-Fries: Rehydrated fish maw can be stir-fried with vegetables and sauces to create a flavorful and texturally interesting dish. It pairs well with a variety of vegetables and meats

Health Tonics: Fish maw is believed to have certain health benefits, such as promoting good skin, improving joint health, and supporting the immune system. As a result, it is sometimes used in the preparation of health tonics and nourishing broths

Deep-Fried Snacks: Fish maw can be deep-fried to create crunchy snacks. After deep-frying, it transforms into a crispy and airy texture, making it a unique addition to a snack or appetizer menu

Hot Pot: Fish maw is a popular ingredient in hot pot dishes. Its absorbent nature allows it to take on the flavors of the hot pot broth, contributing to the overall taste of the dish
