Cardamom is a spice that originates from the seed pods of various plants in the ginger family. It has a strong, unique taste with an intensely aromatic fragrance, making it a popular ingredient in many dishes

There are two main types of cardamom: green cardamom and black cardamom. Both varieties are used in different cuisines around the world

Green cardamom, also known as true cardamom, is the more common and popular variety. It has a strong, pungent flavor with hints of lemon, mint, and smoke, coupled with a refreshing aroma. Green cardamom is often used in sweet dishes, such as desserts and pastries, as well as in some savory dishes

Black cardamom, on the other hand, has a distinctly smoky flavor with a cooling eucalyptus and camphor aroma. It is often used in savory dishes to add a deep and earthy flavor to meat and rice dishes

Cardamom seeds are often ground and used as a spice, or the entire pods may be added to dishes to infuse their flavor. This versatile spice is also used in beverages such as tea, coffee, contributing its unique and exotic essence to the overall flavor profile

Additionally, cardamom is known for its potential health benefits, including digestive and metabolic support, as well as its antimicrobial properties
