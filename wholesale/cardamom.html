Cardamom Uses:


Cardamom is used in various forms, including whole pods, seeds, or ground powder. It's a key ingredient in many traditional dishes, such as curries, rice dishes, stews, and desserts

In addition to its culinary uses, cardamom is also known for its potential health benefits. It has been used in traditional medicine for its digestive and anti-inflammatory properties

Culinary Uses: Cardamom is used to flavor beverages like tea and coffee, as well as desserts like cakes, cookies, and ice cream. It's also a common ingredient in spice blends and masalas used in curries and stews

Medicinal Uses: Cardamom has been used in various traditional medicine systems to aid digestion, relieve nausea, and alleviate respiratory issues. It's also believed to have antibacterial and antioxidant properties

Aromatic Uses: The pleasant aroma of cardamom makes it a popular choice for adding fragrance to perfumes, incense, and potpourri
