Merbau Timber Uses:


Merbau wood is highly valued for its durability, attractive appearance, and versatility, making it suitable for various applications in construction and woodworking

Decking: Merbau is a popular choice for outdoor decking due to its natural resistance to decay, insects, and moisture. Its durability and rich color make it an excellent option for creating long-lasting, attractive decks

Flooring: Merbau is frequently used for both residential and commercial flooring. Its hardness and beautiful grain patterns make it an ideal choice for high-traffic areas

Furniture: Merbau wood is used to craft indoor and outdoor furniture pieces, including tables, chairs, benches, and cabinets. Its durability ensures that furniture made from Merbau can withstand heavy use and exposure to the elements

Boat Building: Due to its resistance to moisture and decay, Merbau wood is a common choice for boat building. It can be used for hull construction, decking, and interior components in marine applications

Stairs and Handrails: The hardness and stability of Merbau make it a suitable material for constructing stairs and handrails in homes and commercial buildings

Doors and Windows: Merbau wood is used to make doors and window frames because of its durability and resistance to warping, which helps maintain the structural integrity of these components

Cabinetry: Merbau is employed in the production of high-quality cabinetry, providing a beautiful and sturdy option for kitchen and bathroom cabinets

Outdoor Structures: Merbau is used for constructing pergolas, gazebos, and outdoor pavilions, thanks to its resistance to weathering and insects

Decorative Woodworking: Craftsmen use Merbau wood for various decorative woodworking projects, including carvings, moldings, and intricate detailing

Fencing: Merbau can be used for fencing, providing an attractive and durable option for property boundaries
