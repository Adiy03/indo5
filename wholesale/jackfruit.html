Jackfruit is a tropical fruit known for its large size, unique appearance. It is native to South and Southeast Asia

The flesh of a ripe jackfruit is sweet and has a unique tropical flavor. It is often described as a combination of banana, pineapple, and mango

However, unripe or green jackfruit has a neutral taste and a fibrous texture that makes it an excellent candidate for mimicking the texture of pulled chicken when shredded

Ripe jackfruit can be enjoyed in various ways: Fresh, Smoothies, Desserts, candies, jams, and snacks. Ripe jackfruit can also be used in cooked dishes like curries or stews

Young green jackfruit, in particular, has a neutral taste and fibrous texture that makes it a suitable replacement for meat in many recipes. It can be shredded and used in dishes like vegan pulled chicken, BBQ sandwiches, tacos, and more

In Indonesia, jackfruit often referred to as "nangka" in Bahasa Indonesia, is a popular tropical fruit in Indonesia and other Southeast Asian countries. Indonesia is one of the largest producers of jackfruit in the world

Indonesian jackfruit is typically large, with a spiky green or yellowish-green outer skin. When ripe, the fruit turns yellow or brown and has a fragrant aroma
