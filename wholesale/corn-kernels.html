Corn kernels refer to the individual seed units found on a corn cob, which is the edible part of the corn plant

Corn Kernels Uses:


Snack Foods: Corn kernels are used to produce snack foods such as tortilla chips, popcorn, and corn-based snacks

Corn Syrup: Corn kernels are processed to extract corn syrup, which is used as a sweetener in a wide range of products such as soft drinks, candies, and baked goods

Cornmeal and Corn Flour: Ground corn kernels are used to produce cornmeal and corn flour, which are used in baking, cooking, and as a base for various dishes

Cereals and Breakfast Foods: Corn is used in the production of breakfast cereals and various breakfast foods

Processed Foods: Corn derivatives like cornstarch and corn oil are used in the production of processed foods for thickening, frying, and flavoring

Livestock and Animal Feed: Corn kernels are a major component of animal feed, providing a valuable source of energy and nutrients for livestock and poultry
