Durians. HS Code: 081060

Durian is a tropical fruit known for its distinctive large size, thorn-covered husk, and strong aroma, which is often described as a combination of sweet, savory, and creamy notes

Durian has a large, spiky, and hard outer husk that encases the edible flesh. The color of the husk can vary from green to brown, depending on the ripeness of the fruit. Inside, the edible flesh is custard-like and pale yellow to red in color, surrounding several large seeds

Durian is renowned for its powerful and pungent smell, which can be off-putting to some people but is beloved by others. The odor is often described as strong and distinctive, with a combination of sweet, savory, and sometimes even sulfurous notes

The taste of durian can vary depending on the variety, but it is generally rich, sweet, and creamy, with a slightly bitter undertone. The texture of the flesh is soft and custard-like, making it a unique sensory experience for those who enjoy it

Durian is commonly consumed fresh, with its flesh being the primary edible part. It is also used as an ingredient in various desserts, sweets, and savory dishes in Southeast Asian cuisine. Durian-flavored products, such as candies, cakes, and ice cream, are also popular in many parts of the world

Indonesia is one of the top durian-producing countries globally, with significant cultivation in regions such as Sumatra, Java, Kalimantan, and Sulawesi. The country's tropical climate and fertile soil provide favorable conditions for durian cultivation, contributing to the abundant production of high-quality fruits
