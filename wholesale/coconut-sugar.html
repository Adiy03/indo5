While both are derived from palm trees, coconut sugar and palm sugar differ in taste, aroma, and the source of their base ingredients used in production

Coconut sugar is made from sap or latex extracted from coconut blossoms. It comes from the coconut tree. Palm sugar is made from sap or latex extracted from various types of palm trees, not just coconuts. It can come from various species of palm trees, such as the arenga palm (Arenga pinnata)

Coconut sugar has a distinct caramel flavor with a hint of butterscotch. Palm sugar has a more complex flavor, often with caramel notes and sometimes hints of fruit or specific palm plant flavors, depending on the species of palm tree used

Coconut Sugar Used as a sweetener in various dishes and beverages and as a substitute for white or brown sugar. However besides being used as a sweetener, palm sugar is often used in cuisine to impart its unique flavor to dishes like sauces and curries
