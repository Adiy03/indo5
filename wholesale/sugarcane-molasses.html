Sugarcane molasses is a byproduct of the sugar refining process. It's a thick, dark syrupy substance that remains after sugar crystals are extracted from sugarcane juice

It has a strong, distinct flavor that is both sweet and slightly bitter

Sugarcane Molasses Uses:


Baking and Cooking: Sugarcane molasses adds a unique flavor and moisture to baked goods such as gingerbread, cookies, muffins, and cakes. It's also used to enhance the depth of flavor in savory dishes, including marinades, glazes, and sauces for meats and vegetables

Sweetener: Molasses serves as a natural alternative to traditional sugars. Its distinct taste makes it a great choice for sweetening beverages like tea or coffee, and it can be used as a topping for pancakes, waffles, and other breakfast items

Candies and Confections: Molasses is a key ingredient in making traditional candies like taffy, toffee, and caramel due to its rich flavor and consistency

Supplement and Nutrition: Blackstrap molasses, in particular, is often consumed as a nutritional supplement due to its relatively high content of minerals like iron, calcium, potassium, and magnesium. Some people incorporate it into their diets for potential health benefits

Health and Wellness: Some people believe that blackstrap molasses can offer potential health benefits, such as supporting bone health, aiding digestion, and helping with iron deficiency due to its iron content
