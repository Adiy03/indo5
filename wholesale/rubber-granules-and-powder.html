Rubber granules and rubber powder are materials derived from recycled rubber products, typically tires, that have undergone a process to break down the rubber into smaller particles. These materials find various applications across industries due to their unique properties and environmental benefits

Rubber Granules Uses:


Rubber granules are small particles of rubber, usually ranging in size from 1 to 5 millimeters. They are commonly used in applications where their resilience, flexibility, and durability are valuable. Some common uses of rubber granules include:

Industrial Flooring: Rubber granules are incorporated into industrial flooring to provide anti-fatigue properties, noise reduction, and resistance to chemicals and heavy machinery

Playground Surfaces: Rubber granules are often used to create safer playground surfaces, such as rubber mulch or poured-in-place rubber, which provide cushioning and reduce the risk of injuries from falls

Athletic Tracks and Sports Fields: Synthetic athletic tracks and sports fields are made using rubber granules mixed with binding agents to create surfaces that are shock-absorbent and provide better traction

Safety Flooring: Rubber granules are used to make safety flooring for gyms, weight rooms, and other areas where impact resistance and slip resistance are important

Equestrian Surfaces: Rubber granules can be mixed with other materials to create surfaces for horse riding arenas that offer cushioning and minimize dust

Rubber Powder Uses:


Rubber powder is a finer material produced from grinding or pulverizing rubber granules further into smaller particles

The size of rubber powder particles can vary, typically ranging from a few micrometers to several millimeters. Rubber powder has a wide range of applications, including:

Tire Manufacturing: Rubber powder can be used as a filler material in the production of new tires, improving their performance characteristics

Asphalt Modification: Rubber powder can be added to asphalt mixtures to enhance road pavement properties, such as durability, flexibility, and resistance to cracking

Rubberized Coatings: Rubber powder is used to create coatings that provide anti-slip properties and durability, often applied to surfaces like decks, roofs, and walkways

Construction Products: Rubber powder can be incorporated into various construction materials like sealants, adhesives, and concrete to improve their properties

Thermal Insulation: Rubber powder is sometimes used as an insulating material due to its ability to reduce heat transfer

Both rubber granules and rubber powder play a significant role in recycling efforts, as they provide a valuable way to repurpose used rubber products like tires, reducing environmental waste and conserving resources
