Onions & shallots, fresh. HS Code: 070310

Shallots are a type of small, elongated onion with a mild, sweet flavor. They are used in cooking to add depth and flavor to various dishes

Shallots typically have a copper, reddish, or gray skin and white flesh that is often tinged with magenta or purple

Shallots Uses:


Cooking Base: Shallots are often used as a flavor base in cooking. They can be finely chopped or minced and used as a starting point for many dishes, similar to onions

Sauteing and Frying: Shallots are suitable for sautéing and frying due to their milder flavor compared to regular onions. They add a subtle oniony taste to dishes without overwhelming the other flavors

Salads: Finely chopped or sliced shallots can be added to salads, providing a mild onion flavor and a bit of crunch. They work well in vinaigrettes and salad dressings

Marinades and Sauces: Shallots are commonly used in marinades and sauces to add depth of flavor. They pair well with various herbs, vinegars, and liquids, enhancing the overall taste of the dish

Dressings and Condiments: Shallots can be included in various dressings and condiments, such as mustard-based sauces, mayonnaise, or compound butters, to add a subtle oniony note

Roasting and Grilling: Whole or halved shallots can be roasted or grilled alongside other vegetables or meats, imparting a sweet and savory flavor to the dish

Caramelizing: Caramelized shallots can be used as a topping for dishes like burgers, pizzas, or tarts, adding a sweet and rich flavor

Pickling: Shallots can be pickled and used as a condiment or garnish. Pickled shallots add a tangy and slightly sweet element to various dishes

Seafood Dishes: Shallots are commonly used in seafood dishes due to their mild flavor. They pair well with various types of fish and shellfish

French Cuisine: Shallots are a staple in French cuisine, where they are often featured in classic sauces like Béarnaise and Bordelaise

Asian Cuisine: Shallots are widely used in Asian cuisine, especially in Southeast Asian dishes. They are often found in stir-fries, curries, and various sauces

Garnish: Finely chopped shallots can be used as a garnish for various dishes, adding both flavor and visual appeal

Compound Butters: Shallots can be incorporated into compound butters, which are flavored butters used to enhance the taste of grilled meats, vegetables, or bread

Herb Blends: Shallots are often combined with other herbs and spices to create flavorful herb blends for seasoning dishes
