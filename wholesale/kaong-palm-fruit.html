Kaong, also known as sugar palm fruit, is a type of fruit derived from the sugar palm tree (Arenga pinnata). It is native to Southeast Asia and is commonly found in countries such as Indonesia

The fruit is small, round, and typically about the size of a marble. It has a hard outer shell and a fleshy, translucent, and jelly-like interior

Kaong fruit is known for its sweet taste and is often used in various desserts and beverages. It is commonly used as a topping for shaved ice desserts, mixed with other tropical fruits, or incorporated into sweet beverages and cocktails. In some cultures, kaong is also fermented to produce a sweet alcoholic beverage
