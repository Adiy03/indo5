Indonesia Wholesale Trade: Tapioca Starch Flour for Sale


Tapioca. HS Code: 190300

Tapioca starch, also known as tapioca flour, is a starchy substance extracted from the root of the cassava plant

It is a versatile ingredient used in various culinary applications due to its unique properties

Tapioca Starch Flour Uses:


Thickening Agent: Tapioca starch is often used as a thickening agent in both sweet and savory dishes. It has a higher gelatinization temperature than cornstarch, making it suitable for thickening hot mixtures without risk of breaking down

Gluten-Free Baking: Tapioca starch is a popular ingredient in gluten-free baking. It is used in combination with other gluten-free flours to improve the texture of baked goods, providing a light and airy quality

Puddings and Desserts: Tapioca starch is a key ingredient in traditional tapioca pudding. It gives the pudding its characteristic texture with small, chewy pearls. Tapioca starch can also be used in various other desserts to achieve a smooth and glossy consistency

Pearl Tapioca: Tapioca starch is processed into small, round pearls known as tapioca pearls. These pearls are used in the preparation of bubble tea, pudding, and various desserts

Binding Agent: Tapioca starch can be used as a binding agent in recipes such as veggie burgers, meatballs, and gluten-free baked goods

Glaze and Sauce Thicker: Tapioca starch can be used to thicken glazes and sauces. It imparts a glossy finish to the final product

Coating for Fried Foods: Tapioca starch can be used as a coating for frying foods. It creates a crispy and light texture on the outside of items like chicken, fish, or vegetables

Dairy-Free Alternatives: Tapioca starch is often used in dairy-free and vegan recipes to achieve creamy textures in sauces, puddings, and soups

Asian Cuisine: Tapioca starch is widely used in Asian cuisine for various applications. It is a common ingredient in the preparation of noodles, dumplings, and as a thickening agent in soups and sauces

Texture Modifier: Tapioca starch can be used to modify the texture of certain foods, making them smoother and more palatable. It is often used in gluten-free flour blends for this purpose

Dusting Agent: Tapioca starch can be used as a dusting agent to prevent sticking when rolling out dough or working with sticky ingredients

Frozen Desserts: Tapioca starch is sometimes used in frozen desserts like ice cream to improve texture and prevent ice crystals from forming
