Mung beans, also known as green gram, are a type of legume that belongs to the Fabaceae family. Mung beans are known for their nutritional richness and versatility in cooking

Mung beans are a rich source of nutrients, including protein, fiber, vitamins, and minerals. They are known for their high protein content, making them a popular dietary staple, especially in vegetarian and vegan diets

Mung beans are used in various culinary preparations. They can be sprouted and added to salads, used as a filling in savory dishes, or ground into flour for making pancakes and crepes. In Asian cuisines, mung beans are commonly used in soups, curries, and sweet dishes

Mung beans are utilized in the food industry for producing various products, including bean sprouts, noodles, and snacks. Mung bean starch and flour are also used in the production of desserts and as a thickening agent in various food products
