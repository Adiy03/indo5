Wood pellets are a type of biomass fuel made from compressed wood or sawdust

They are typically cylindrical in shape and have a small diameter, often around 6-8 millimeters. Wood pellets are commonly used as a renewable and environmentally friendly source of heat and energy

Wood Pellets Uses :


Residential Heating: Wood pellets are frequently used in residential pellet stoves and pellet boilers for space heating. These appliances are designed to burn pellets efficiently and provide a source of warmth for homes. They are known for their convenience and clean-burning characteristics

Commercial and Industrial Heating: Many commercial and industrial facilities use wood pellet boilers and furnaces for heating. These systems can provide heat for large spaces, such as warehouses, schools, and factories

Electricity Generation: Wood pellets can be burned in power plants to generate electricity. This is especially common in regions where biomass power generation is promoted as a renewable energy source

Combined Heat and Power (CHP) Systems: Some facilities use wood pellet CHP systems, which produce both electricity and heat simultaneously. These systems are highly efficient and are often used in district heating schemes

Hot Water Production: Wood pellet boilers can also be used to produce hot water for domestic use, such as for showers and faucets. This is common in residential and small-scale commercial applications

Pellet Grills: Wood pellet grills and smokers use wood pellets as a fuel source for outdoor cooking. They provide a distinctive smoky flavor to grilled foods and are known for their precise temperature control

Animal Bedding: Wood pellets can be used as bedding material for animals, such as horses, poultry, and small pets. They provide a comfortable and absorbent surface for animals while also being easy to clean

Cat Litter: Some cat litters are made from compressed wood pellets. They are absorbent and help control odors

Pelletized Horse Bedding: Wood pellets can also be specifically designed for use as horse bedding. They are highly absorbent and can reduce the amount of bedding material needed in horse stalls

Pellet Fuel for Biomass Boilers: In some regions, large-scale biomass boilers in institutions like schools or hospitals use wood pellets as a fuel source for centralized heating systems

Industrial Processes: Wood pellets can be used as a source of heat for various industrial processes, such as drying and curing in the manufacturing of wood products, textiles, and food

Greenhouse Heating: Wood pellet boilers are used in greenhouse operations to provide consistent heating for plants during colder months
