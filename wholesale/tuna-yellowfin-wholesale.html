Tuna (yellowfin), frozen. HS Code: 030342

Yellowfin tuna (Thunnus albacares) is a species of tuna found in tropical and subtropical oceans worldwide

Yellowfin tuna are easily identified by their streamlined body, metallic dark blue to black back, yellow sides, and a silver belly

Yellowfin Tuna Uses:


Yellowfin tuna is prized for its firm texture and rich flavor, making it a popular choice for raw fish dishes like sushi and sashimi. The high-quality, deep-red flesh is often preferred for these delicacies

Yellowfin tuna is also used in the production of canned tuna. The meat is processed, cooked, and canned for widespread consumption. Canned tuna is a convenient and versatile product used in various dishes, such as salads, sandwiches, and casseroles

Yellowfin Tuna Forms:


Whole Yellowfin Tuna: This form involves exporting the entire fish as a whole, either fresh or frozen. Whole yellowfin tuna is often preferred by some markets, especially those with demand for fresh fish

Yellowfin Tuna Loins: Tuna loins are boneless and skinless cuts of meat taken from the back of the fish. Yellowfin tuna loins are a popular export product, particularly for high-end markets and sushi/sashimi consumption

Yellowfin Tuna Steaks: - Tuna steaks are cross-sectional cuts from the fish, typically with the skin and bones removed. These steaks are often sold fresh or frozen and are suitable for various cooking methods

Yellowfin Tuna Fillets: - Fillets are boneless cuts of fish meat. While tuna is not traditionally filleted like some other fish, the term is sometimes used to describe processed cuts of yellowfin tuna. These fillets are often used in the canned tuna industry

Canned Yellowfin Tuna: - Canned yellowfin tuna is a widely exported product. The tuna is processed, cooked, and then canned with various options such as in water, oil, or with added flavors. Canned tuna is a convenient and versatile form of consumption

Saku Blocks: - Saku blocks are rectangular blocks of tuna meat, usually cut from the loins. They are popular in the sushi and sashimi market, as they are easy to handle and slice into uniform portions
