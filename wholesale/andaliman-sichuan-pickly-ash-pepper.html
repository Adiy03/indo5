Andaliman, also known as Batak pepper or Cimbuak pepper, is a type of spice originating from North Sumatra, Indonesia

Andaliman has a unique flavor profile, described as citrusy, tangy, and slightly numbing. Its distinctive taste can appeal to consumers and chefs looking for exotic and rare flavors

Andaliman and Sichuan pepper (or also known as Szechuan pepper or Chinese pepper) are two different spices but are often confused because they have some similarities in taste and culinary use

Despite being called "pepper," andaliman is not actually a member of the pepper family (Piperaceae), which includes black and white pepper

Andaliman is often used to impart a distinctive flavor to meat, fish, vegetables, and other dishes. Andaliman can be used as a primary spice or ground and sprinkled on dishes before serving

Andaliman can be included in spice blends to create a distinctive flavor profile

Andaliman is sometimes used to prepare condiments and seasonings. It can be ground and added to sauces, marinades, or spice mixes to infuse a citrusy and numbing flavor

In some culinary experiments, andaliman has been incorporated into beverages, providing a unique and exotic twist to drinks. This usage is not as common as in food recipes

While not as popular as other spices like black or white pepper, andaliman has started to gain attention in the international market, especially among culinary enthusiasts seeking exotic spices
