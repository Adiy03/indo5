Some of the well-known coffee regions in Indonesia include:

Sumatra Coffe Beans:


Sumatra is famous for producing Arabica coffee beans, with varieties like Mandheling, Lintong, and Gayo. These beans are known for their rich, full-bodied flavors with hints of earthiness, spice, and chocolate

Java Coffe Beans:


Java is another significant coffee-producing region in Indonesia. The island of Java produces both Arabica and Robusta coffee beans. Javanese coffee often has a smooth, mild taste and is sometimes blended with other beans in coffee blends

Sulawesi (formerly known as Celebes):


Sulawesi is known for producing Toraja coffee, which is grown in the highlands of the island. Toraja coffee typically has a complex flavor profile, with notes of fruit, spice, and herbs

Bali Coffe Beans:


Bali is known for producing Arabica coffee, which is typically grown in the volcanic highlands. Balinese coffee tends to have a mild, sweet taste with subtle acidity

Flores Coffe Beans:


Flores is an emerging coffee region in Indonesia, known for producing Arabica coffee with unique flavor profiles that can vary depending on the microclimates of different areas

Papua Coffe Beans:


Papua is one of the newest coffee-producing regions in Indonesia. The coffee from this region is gaining recognition for its distinctive flavors and high-quality Arabica beans

Indonesia also produces Robusta coffee beans, which are commonly used in espresso blends and instant coffee due to their higher caffeine content and stronger, more bitter flavor
