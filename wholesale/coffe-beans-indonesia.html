Indonesia Coffe Beans:


Sumatra Mandheling: Sumatra, an island in western Indonesia, is famous for its Mandheling coffee. These beans are known for their full body, low acidity, and earthy, herbal, and spicy flavor notes. They are often used in espresso blends

Java: Java, another Indonesian island, has a long history of coffee cultivation. Java coffee is known for its medium body and a slightly sweet, bright flavor with mild acidity

Sulawesi (formerly Celebes): Coffee from Sulawesi has a unique taste profile with a combination of fruity and spicy notes. It is often grown in the Toraja region and is known as Toraja coffee

Bali Kintamani: Coffee from the Kintamani highlands in Bali is gaining recognition for its bright acidity, medium body, and citrusy, fruity flavors. It's often grown using traditional methods and is considered an organic coffee

Papua New Guinea: While not part of Indonesia, Papua New Guinea, which shares the island of New Guinea with Indonesia's Papua province, is a significant coffee producer. The beans from this region are known for their medium body and unique flavor profile, often featuring fruity and herbal notes

Flores: Coffee from the Flores island in Indonesia has gained popularity for its well-balanced flavor, medium body, and hints of floral and nutty notes

Indonesian coffee is typically grown in volcanic soil and often undergoes a unique wet-hulling process known as "Giling Basah," which contributes to its distinctive flavor profiles

The country's diverse geography and climate create a wide range of coffee flavors, making Indonesian coffee beans highly sought after by coffee enthusiasts and roasters around the world

In recent years, Indonesian coffee producers have made efforts to improve the quality and sustainability of their coffee production, and many Indonesian coffees are now certified as organic or grown using environmentally friendly practices
