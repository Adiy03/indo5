Guava Uses:


Fresh Eating: The most straightforward use of guavas is to eat them fresh. Simply wash, cut, and enjoy the sweet, juicy flesh. You can scoop out the seeds or eat them along with the flesh

Guava Juice: Guavas are often used to make delicious and refreshing guava juice. You can blend the fruit with water and a sweetener (if desired) to make homemade guava juice

Jams and Jellies: Guavas have natural pectin, which makes them excellent for making jams, jellies, and preserves. These can be spread on toast, used as a topping for desserts, or incorporated into pastries

Desserts: Guavas can be used in various dessert recipes. They can be added to fruit salads, ice creams, sorbets, and fruit tarts for their unique flavor and sweetness

Smoothies: Guavas are a popular addition to smoothies and fruit blends. They add a tropical twist and natural sweetness to your beverages

Guava Paste: Guava paste is a concentrated guava product made by cooking down guava pulp with sugar. It's often used in Latin American and Caribbean cuisine, either on its own or as an ingredient in desserts and savory dishes

Candied Guava: Guava slices or cubes can be candied by cooking them in sugar syrup. Candied guava is often used as a garnish for desserts and cakes
