A pomelo is a large citrus fruit that is native to Southeast Asia. It is one of the four original citrus species from which most other citrus fruits

Pomelos are known for their refreshing and mildly sweet flavor, and they are often enjoyed fresh, either peeled and segmented or used in salads and desserts. They are also used to make various juices and jams

Pomelo Uses:


Pomelos have several uses in culinary and non-culinary contexts due to their unique flavor, fragrance, and nutritional value

Fresh Consumption: Pomelos are often eaten fresh by peeling the thick rind and separating the flesh into segments. They have a sweet and mildly tangy flavor, making them a refreshing snack or dessert

Salad Ingredient: Pomelo segments can be added to salads for a burst of citrusy flavor and juicy texture. They pair well with ingredients like shrimp, avocado, greens, and vinaigrette dressings

Juice: Pomelo juice is made by blending the flesh with a little water and sugar, if desired. It can be enjoyed as a refreshing beverage on its own or used as a base for cocktails and mocktails

Jam and Marmalade: Pomelos can be turned into delicious jams, preserves, or marmalades. The natural sweetness and tanginess of the fruit make it an excellent choice for homemade spreads

Candied Pomelo Peel: The thick pomelo rind can be candied by boiling it in sugar syrup. The candied peel can be used as a garnish for desserts or enjoyed as a sweet treat

Chutney: Pomelos can be used to make chutneys, which are condiments that pair well with savory dishes. Pomelo chutney often includes ingredients like spices, onions, and vinegar

Cooking: While not as commonly used in cooking as other citrus fruits, pomelos can be incorporated into recipes. For example, you can use pomelo zest or juice to flavor sauces, marinades, and dressings
