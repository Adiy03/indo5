Plywood, veneered panels and similar laminated wood. HS Code: 4412

Plywood is a type of engineered wood product made from thin layers of wood veneer, or "plies," that are glued together to form a panel. These layers are usually placed at right angles to one another to increase the strength and stability of the wood

Plywood is known for its versatility, durability, and strength, and it finds application in various construction, furniture, and interior design projects

Faced film plywood, also known as film-faced plywood, is a type of plywood that has been covered with a durable and often waterproof film on both sides. This film provides additional protection to the plywood, making it more resistant to moisture, abrasion, and other forms of damage

Faced film plywood is commonly used in construction, particularly for projects that require materials to withstand harsh environmental conditions or repeated exposure to moisture

Both plywood and faced film plywood are widely used in the construction industry for various applications, including roofing, flooring, walls, and concrete formwork. Their strength, versatility, and structural integrity make them suitable for use in various building and renovation projects

Plywood is commonly used in the manufacturing of furniture, cabinetry, and other interior design elements. Its smooth surface and structural stability make it a preferred material for creating durable and aesthetically pleasing furniture pieces

Plywood is often used in DIY projects and carpentry work due to its ease of use and versatility. It can be easily cut, shaped, and assembled, making it an ideal material for various woodworking projects

Faced film plywood is particularly suitable for exterior applications where moisture resistance and durability are essential. It is often used in outdoor construction projects, such as building facades, exterior walls, and outdoor furniture, where exposure to the elements is a concern

Both plywood and faced film plywood offer practical and reliable solutions for a wide range of construction and woodworking needs, providing durability, stability, and a smooth surface finish for various applications in the building and design industries
