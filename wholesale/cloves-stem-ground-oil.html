Cloves are aromatic flower buds commonly used as a spice in cooking and traditional medicine. They come from the Syzygium aromaticum tree, which is native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia

Cloves Uses:


Whole Cloves: These are the dried flower buds of the clove tree. Whole cloves are used in various culinary applications and are often added to dishes for their strong, warm, and spicy flavor

They are commonly used in soups, stews, curries, and pickles. They can also be used to stud hams or citrus fruits for added flavor

Ground Cloves: Ground cloves are made by grinding whole cloves into a fine powder. They are used as a spice in baking and cooking

Ground cloves are a key ingredient in many spice blends, such as pumpkin spice and garam masala. They are used in sweets, bread, and spiced beverages like mulled wine and chai tea

Cloves Oil: Clove essential oil is extracted from the clove buds. It is used in aromatherapy for its warm and soothing aroma

Clove oil is also used in dental care products like toothpaste and mouthwash due to its natural antiseptic properties. In some cultures, it's used as a traditional remedy for toothaches
