Mutton refers to the meat of mature sheep. The term "mutton" specifically distinguishes the meat of older sheep from "lamb," which refers to the meat of younger sheep

Mutton has a stronger flavor compared to lamb and can sometimes be tougher in texture due to the age of the animal

Mutton Uses:


Mutton Kebabs: Mutton can be minced and combined with spices, herbs, and other flavorings to make delicious kebabs that can be grilled or cooked on skewers

Mutton Curry: Mutton is commonly used in curries across different cuisines. Indian and Pakistani cuisine, for example, feature various mutton curry recipes like Rogan Josh, Bhuna Gosht, and Korma

Mutton Sausages: Ground mutton can be used to make sausages, which can be grilled, fried, or used in various dishes

Shepherd's Pie: A classic dish that includes minced or diced mutton cooked with vegetables, topped with mashed potatoes, and baked until golden

Mutton Stew: Mutton can be slow-cooked in a stew with vegetables, herbs, and spices, resulting in a hearty and flavorful dish

Mutton Biryani: A fragrant rice dish made with marinated mutton pieces, aromatic spices, and basmati rice. It's often garnished with fried onions, nuts, and fresh herbs

Mutton Roast: Mutton can be roasted with herbs, spices, and sometimes a marinade to create a succulent and flavorful roasted dish

Mutton Tacos or Wraps: Cooked and seasoned mutton can be used as a filling for tacos, wraps, or burritos, along with various toppings and sauces

Mutton Soup: Mutton bones and meat can be used to make flavorful broths and soups, often enriched with vegetables and legumes

Mutton Pies: Mutton can be used as a filling for savory pies, either in a minced form or with larger chunks, along with vegetables and a hearty sauce

Mutton is often used in various cuisines around the world and can be prepared in numerous ways, such as roasting, grilling, stewing, and currying. In some cultures, mutton is considered a delicacy and is used in traditional dishes and recipes
