Kapok. HS Code: 14049030

Kapok is a natural fiber obtained from the seed pods of the kapok tree, which is native to tropical regions

The kapok tree is known for its large, spreading canopy and distinctive seed pods that contain a fluffy, cotton-like substance. This fiber is sometimes referred to as "silk cotton" due to its silky texture and cotton-like appearance

One of the primary uses of kapok fiber is as a stuffing material for pillows, mattresses, and upholstery. Its lightweight, resilient, and buoyant nature makes it an ideal natural alternative to synthetic fillers, providing comfort and support in various household and commercial products

Kapok fiber has excellent insulating properties, making it suitable for use in life jackets, sleeping bags, and other thermal insulation products. Its water-resistant and quick-drying characteristics also make it ideal for outdoor gear and equipment used in wet environments

Due to its natural buoyancy, kapok fiber is used in the production of life-saving equipment such as lifebuoys and life rafts. Its ability to provide adequate buoyancy even when wet has made it a reliable choice for maritime safety applications

Indonesia is one of the major producers of kapok globally, with a significant supply of high-quality kapok fiber. The country's favorable tropical climate and suitable geographical conditions facilitate the cultivation of kapok trees, ensuring a consistent and reliable supply for global market
